About Me

I started my career as an intern at Intel while earning my bachelor’s degree in computer systems engineering (CSE) at Arizona State University (ASU). My goal then was to become a low-level programmer who worked with microcontrollers, but life had different plans for me. At Intel, I was a Platform Engineering and Development intern who worked with computer graphics by managing and developing test environments, measuring graphics performance on these test environments, and finally validating the data. I did this for one year before I transferred to becoming a Software Quality Engineer (SQE).

This is where my data analyst journey begins. As an SQE, I was tasked with a few unconventional tasks: one of them had to do with creating and managing metrics in Excel while another was to manage the databases the data came from. When I was first given these tasks, I had no idea what I was looking at. I had no idea what SQL was, what the graphs meant, or how to interact with the databases. I was completely in the dark on everything. It took a while but I did figure it all out on my own in time.

I had finally managed to learn enough SQL to do my job, some advanced Excel skills, how to interact with and manage the databases, and everything else in between to make the metrics teams required when we switched from using Excel to Power BI. Now I had a new tool to learn, and it was fun. I found a new challenge for myself and I dove head first into it, learning by watching YouTube videos, searching Google for answers, and just good old fashion trial and error. I worked hard to convert what we did in Excel to what we needed in Power BI and it showed because I eventually became one of the Power BI experts who trained teammates and got asked ad-hoc requests.

Because of my love for data analytics and database management, I was eventually transferred to a Development and Operations Engineer (DevOps) role. Basically, I changed position names but kept doing the same type of work. This is where I thrived. I loved working with Power BI and SQL and managing the databases. I loved being a go-to person for people with questions about all three and sharing my knowledge with others. And I loved taking on new challenges that put my knowledge to the test.

And now here I am. Working on learning more about data analytics, such as CTE and Window Functions in SQL, Pandas and NumPy in Python, and R. I’m actually going back to school to get my master’s degree in Data Science, Analytics, and Engineering (DSAE) from ASU fall of 2026, and I plan to start with a visualization class (because what good is data if the visuals can’t tell a story?). So keep up with me here and see how I learn and expand my knowledge with new projects!

Oh, I’m not just a data analyst either. Although I enjoy brushing up on my skills in my spare time, I also play piano and sing, crochet and knit, play video games, and like to draw. I am an animal mom to one very spoiled cat, an aunt to more spoiled animals and children, and I help take care of neo-natal kittens when possible.
